I'm a B.Tech CSE graduate from IES IPS Academy, Indore (CGPA 8.14) — but my real passion has always been words. While my degree gave me a technical foundation, it was writing that chose me. From SEO blogs to Urdu ghazals, I've spent years crafting content that connects people with ideas.

## How It Started ✍️

My writing journey began quietly — with a love for Urdu poetry. I wrote ghazals and shayari long before I knew what "content writing" meant professionally. Words were my way of making sense of the world.

When I started writing SEO blogs at **Earningtricks.in** in 2020, I discovered that the same discipline it takes to craft a perfect sher (couplet) — precision, intent, economy of words — applies directly to writing content that ranks on Google.

I wrote **2–3 blogs every single day** for two years. That volume taught me speed without sacrificing quality, and gave me an intuition for what readers actually want to read.

## Growing Into Digital Marketing 📈

At **Cyber Infrastructure Pvt. Ltd.** (Sept 2024 – April 2025), I expanded from writing into the full spectrum of digital marketing. I handled on-page and off-page SEO, ran backlink outreach campaigns, wrote case studies, managed social media content for Instagram, LinkedIn, and Twitter — and even managed a team of interns.

Working with Google Analytics, Search Console, SEMrush, and Ahrefs daily taught me that great content isn't just beautifully written — it's strategically placed, properly optimized, and continuously measured.

## Content Writer Executive 💼

Currently at **Digitech India** (April 2025 – Present), I write SEO blogs, social media posts, service page copy, and video scripts. Every piece of content I create has a purpose: to rank, to convert, or to connect.

## The Other Side — Urdu Poetry 🖊️

Parallel to my professional career runs a quieter creative life. I authored **_Bedili_** — a published collection of Urdu ghazals and shayari. The word *bedili* means indifference or heartlessness — but the verses inside are anything but.

I also contributed original poetry to my college's literary anthology **_Abhivyakti_**, and continue writing ghazals actively.

> Writing poetry made me a better content writer. It taught me the weight of a single word, the power of rhythm, and how simplicity is often the hardest thing to achieve.

## What I Believe About Writing 💡

Good content is invisible. When writing works, the reader doesn't notice the craft — they just feel understood, informed, or inspired. That's the standard I hold myself to, whether I'm writing an SEO blog, a social media caption, or a ghazal.
